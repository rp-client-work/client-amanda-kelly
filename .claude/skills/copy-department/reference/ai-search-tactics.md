# AI-search optimization tactics (AEO / GEO / AIO)

These are baked into architecture, strategy, and drafting on every job — not a separate add-on pass.

**AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)** — getting cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Copilot, Grok:
- Direct-answer passages, 40-60 words, positioned high on the page
- Q&A blocks in natural user phrasing
- Entity clarity in the first 100 words: who the business is, what it does, where it operates, who it serves
- Quotable, self-contained expert lines the models can lift verbatim

**GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)** — lifting citation rate in generative search results:
- Cite authoritative sources inline
- Specific statistics with named sources
- Quoted expert voices
- Unique, precise language — no generic filler
- Authoritative signal phrasing ("studies show," "research indicates," "according to")
- Applied at paragraph level throughout, not just in FAQs

**AIO (AI Overview Optimization)** — getting pulled into Google AI Overviews / AI Mode / SGE:
- Full category-appropriate Schema Stack per page (see schema-stack.md)
- Passage-ready paragraph blocks: short, self-contained, one idea per paragraph
- Tables and bullet lists for comparative or step-based content
- Semantic HTML structure signals noted in copy specs for the developer

Non-negotiable on every initial delivery: the QA gate blocks completion until AEO, GEO, and AIO checks all pass, including full Schema Stack validation.
